# Opera & Opera GX (Android)

## 1. Overview & positioning

- **Opera for Android**: full-featured Chromium/Blink browser positioned as "fast, secure & personal AI browser" — the everything-built-in alternative to Chrome: free VPN, ad blocker, AI assistant, data savings, no extensions needed. Version numbering shares the desktop release train (Opera 133 stable as of July 2026).
- **Opera GX Mobile**: "the world's first mobile browser for gamers," launched at E3 2021. Positioned on aesthetics (gaming themes, sounds, haptics), GX Corner gaming content hub, and cross-device Flow sync. Won an iF Design Award in 2026.
- Opera's Android family also includes **Opera Mini** (proxy-compression data saver, dominant in Africa/emerging markets) and formerly Opera Touch (gesture/FAB browser whose DNA lives on in GX Mobile's Fast Action Button and Flow).
- Business model: default-search deals, ad/partner content on the start page, VPN Pro subscription (~$4/mo), and Web3/fintech (MiniPay stablecoin wallet, 10M+ wallets as of Sept 2025).
- Global mobile share is small but Opera over-indexes in Africa, Eastern Europe, and Southeast Asia on the Mini/data-saver legacy.

## 2. Feature inventory by category

### Navigation & address bar
- Combined address/search bar at top of screen (phone layout).
- Bottom navigation bar with back/forward, Ask AI button (center, since Jan 2026), tab counter, and O-menu button.
- QR/barcode scanner built into the search field.
- Search-engine picker chip in the address bar when typing (Google, Yandex, DuckDuckGo, Amazon, etc.).
- Cryptojacking protection and fraud/malware warnings on navigation.
- Instant search suggestions and "Ask AI" entry directly from the search box (Dec 2025).

### Tabs & tab management
- Three selectable tab-gallery layouts: **carousel** (classic large previews), **grid**, and **list** (most compact) — v89, May 2025, billed as "the most complete tab management system."
- **Tab Islands**: tab grouping by long-press-and-drag one tab onto another; groups can be named and managed; links opened from a page auto-cluster into an island (auto-grouping can be disabled).
- Tab search: magnifying-glass icon in tab gallery, searches page title, site name, or URL words.
- Recently closed tabs: remembers up to 100; restore individually or whole Tab Islands.
- Private tabs in a separate gallery pane; tabs synced from other devices visible via the gallery.

### Home / New-tab page (speed dial, news feed)
- **Speed Dial**: grid of site shortcuts; 2026 update added customizable icon shapes (circle, square, squircle).
- **News feed** below speed dials; channel/interest picker; can be fully disabled. Contains sponsored/ad content.
- 2026 home-screen makeover: weather shortcut at top, **Google AI Mode shortcut under the search bar**, and a FIFA World Cup 2026 live-score dashboard.

### Search & discovery
- Default Google with multi-engine quick switching; private search options.
- Aria/Opera browser AI answers surfaced from the search box.
- Enhanced Aria answers for recipes, shopping, and gaming categories with suggestion chips (v90).

### Privacy & security (free built-in VPN)
- **Free built-in VPN**: no-log, unlimited-bandwidth, no account required; encrypts browser traffic between the device and Opera's VPN servers; hides IP/location. Coarse region choice only: **Americas / Europe / Asia**. Browser-only — does not cover other apps' traffic; technically closer to an encrypted browser proxy than a system VPN (a standard criticism).
- **VPN Pro** (subscription, ~$4/mo): device-wide Android VPN, 3,000+ servers, 48 locations (v90, July 2025), Lightway protocol with post-quantum protection, up to 6 devices. Makes Opera "the only major browser with both free and Pro VPN."
- Cryptomining protection; pop-up blocker; cookie dialog blocker (auto-dismisses GDPR consent banners); tracker blocking bundled with the ad blocker; private browsing mode; passwords/autofill management.

### Ad/content blocking (built-in)
- Native ad blocker (EasyList-based), toggle in O-menu and Settings; per-site exceptions.
- Cookie consent dialog blocker (a distinctive Opera extra).
- Blocking also accelerates pages and reduces data use.
- GX Mobile ships the same built-in ad blocker + cookie dialog blocker.

### Downloads & media
- Full download manager (pause/resume, background downloads, notification controls); long-press to download media; dedicated download button in the built-in video player.
- Offline pages: save full pages for offline reading (distinct from downloads).
- Video downloads and pop-out/picture-in-picture video playback.

### Reading & content
- Night mode with customizable dimming/color temperature that darkens web content (separate from UI dark theme), plus forced-dark for pages.
- Text wrap / text-size controls, forced zoom on any page.
- No full "reader mode" as prominent as Firefox's — a noted gap; Aria page summaries increasingly substitute.

### Customization & appearance (themes, GX styling)
- Opera: light/dark/system app themes; accent color options; speed-dial icon shapes; **bookmark folder emojis** (v90); wallpapers, including **AI-generated wallpapers via Aria**.
- **GX Mobile**: four built-in gaming themes plus community/official **GX Mods** from store.gx.me (287+ mobile mods: themes, wallpapers, splash screens, icons, browser/keyboard sounds, background music, shaders, stickers, fonts); 2026 brought cross-device mod sync and custom shader packs.

### Sync & account (Flow)
- **Flow (My Flow)**: encrypted end-to-end channel between phone and desktop Opera/GX; **no account needed — pair by scanning a QR code**. Share links, notes, images, videos, and files (up to 10 MB per file); acts as a self-chat/temporary shared clipboard.
- Full **Opera account sync** also available: bookmarks, open tabs, history, passwords, speed dials.

### AI features (Aria)
- **Aria → renamed "Opera browser AI" in January 2026**. Free, **no account required**.
- Access: **Ask AI button in the middle of the bottom bar**; also from the search box.
- Capabilities: real-time web-grounded answers with sources; page-context Q&A and **page summarization**; text generation/rewriting; translation in 50+ languages; **image generation** (incl. wallpapers); **image understanding** (camera/gallery uploads); **file analysis** (PDFs/docs); voice input and spoken responses.
- Desktop-side agentic features (Browser Operator, Opera Neon at $19.90/mo) remain desktop-first.

### Gestures & shortcuts (Fast Action Button)
- **Opera GX Mobile — Fast Action Button (FAB)**: persistent thumb-reach button; press-and-slide gestures across two rows — bottom row for search / new tab / close tab / reload, top row for recent tabs — with **vibration and haptic feedback** on each element. GX's signature one-handed navigation (inherited from Opera Touch).
- Opera for Android: long-press back = history; swipe on tab gallery; swipe between speed dial and news feed; pull-to-refresh; no FAB (uses fixed bottom bar).
- Text selection quick actions (search, translate, Ask AI).

### Data saving & performance
- **Data-saver heritage**: Opera Mini's server-side compression (up to 90%) built Opera's emerging-market user base; Opera for Android retains a **data savings mode** toggle that compresses pages through Opera servers.
- Ad blocking as a speed/data feature; GX desktop's RAM/CPU/network limiters do **not** exist on mobile.

### Crypto/Web3 wallet
- Legacy in-browser Crypto Wallet was **phased out of Opera for Android in Dec 2024–2025**.
- Replaced by **MiniPay**: non-custodial dollar-stablecoin wallet on Celo — phone-number-based, ~1-second transfers costing ~$0.001; 10M wallets and 270M transactions by Sept 2025. Focused on Africa/emerging markets.

### Accessibility
- System font-scale respect plus in-browser forced zoom on any page and text-wrap.
- Dark/night modes; TalkBack support; Aria voice input/read-aloud doubles as an accessibility aid.
- Thinner accessibility settings than Samsung Internet.

### Unique extras (GX Corner, sounds)
- **GX Corner** (GX Mobile): gaming hub — daily gaming news, release calendar, **free-game claims and deals aggregator**, personalized game recommendations (2025+).
- **Browser sounds & haptics** (GX Mobile): UI sound effects, keyboard sounds, background music mods; haptics throughout the FAB.
- Opera for Android extras: FIFA World Cup 2026 live dashboard, weather widget, emoji bookmark folders.

## 3. UI/UX structure & feature placement

- **Address bar + bottom bar layout**: Opera: address/search bar at **top**; persistent **bottom bar**: back, forward/search, **Ask AI (center)**, tab counter, O-menu. The true one-hand **FAB design lives in GX Mobile** — a single bottom-center button whose press-and-slide radial rows replace most chrome.
- **Tab switcher design**: Opened via tab-counter button. User-selectable **carousel / grid / list** layouts. Top-right: **tab search**. Left of "+": **recently closed** (up to 100). Separate private-tabs pane. **Tab Islands** shown as grouped clusters; long-press-drag to group. GX Mobile uses a vertical card switcher themed to the active skin.
- **Main menu (the O menu): items in order**
  1. **Controls row (top)**: VPN toggle · Data savings · Ad blocking · Night mode
  2. **Primary items**: Settings · History · Bookmarks · Aria/Opera browser AI · Offline pages · Downloads · Flow
  3. **Shortcut row**: Appearance · Sync · Passwords · Cards · Addresses
  4. **Exit (power) button** at the bottom.
- **Settings: top-level sections in order (approximate)**: Sign in / Opera account → **Start page** (speed dials, news feed, channels) → **Appearance** (theme, layout, address-bar options) → Search engine → Privacy & security (VPN, ad blocker, cookie dialog blocker, tracking, site settings, passwords) → **Browser** (data savings, Tab Islands auto-grouping, text wrap, downloads location) → **Notifications** → **Sync and backup** (incl. Flow/QR pairing) → About/help.
- **Long-press / context menus**: Links: open in new tab / background / private tab / copy / share / download / send to Flow. Images: download, share, copy, search-with, Ask AI. Selected text: copy, search, translate, Ask AI. Speed-dial tiles: edit, move, delete. Tab gallery: long-press-drag to build Tab Islands.
- **Gestures**: Swipe up/down between speed dial and news feed; pull-to-refresh; swipe-to-close in tab gallery; long-press back for history. GX Mobile: FAB press-and-slide gestures with haptic ticks.

## 4. Standout features (what Opera does best)

- **Free, unlimited, no-account VPN inside the browser** — still unique among major browsers; paired with a real device-wide VPN Pro upsell.
- **Everything built-in**: ad blocker + tracker blocker + cookie-dialog blocker + VPN + AI with zero extensions or accounts.
- **Aria / Opera browser AI free with no login** — one of the most complete free mobile AI integrations.
- **Flow**: the slickest no-account phone↔desktop pipeline (QR pairing, E2E-encrypted links/notes/files).
- **Best-in-class mobile tab management** (three layouts, Tab Islands, tab search, 100-tab undo).
- **Data-savings heritage**: compression modes unbeatable on cost-sensitive networks.
- **GX Mobile's personality**: the only mainstream mobile browser with themes+sounds+haptics+FAB one-handed control and a gaming content hub; iF Design Award 2026.

## 5. Weaknesses & common criticisms

- **Chinese-ownership trust deficit**: majority stake held by Kunlun Tech consortium (since 2016); recurring data-sharing concerns despite Norway HQ.
- **"VPN" is really a browser proxy**: free tier protects only Opera traffic, coarse 3-region choice; unsuitable for whole-device protection unless you pay for VPN Pro.
- **Ad-funded start page**: sponsored speed dials, news-feed ads, and partner shortcuts read as bloat; requires manual decluttering.
- **Feature bloat / upsell fatigue**: AI, crypto/MiniPay, World Cup dashboards, VPN Pro promos — "kitchen-sink" criticism; GX's novelty features seen as gimmicks by non-gamers.
- **Slower security patch cadence than Chrome.**
- **No mobile extensions**; no true reader mode; weaker accessibility settings.
- GX Mobile lacks desktop GX's actual utility features (RAM/CPU/network limiters) — largely cosmetic differentiation.
- Data-savings compression routes traffic through Opera servers — a privacy trade-off.
